# A $2,500 mistake and a broken $5,000 MacBook

Last week, I broke my 16-inch M1 Max MacBook with 32 GB of memory and 1 TB of storage after spilling a smoothie on it. The repair cost is painful, but the bigger lesson is even clearer: this did not happen because of one unlucky moment. It happened because I ignored a small risk that I had already seen before.

The smoothie bottle had an insecure lid. I knew it was not reliable, and I had already had problems with it before. Instead of replacing it or being more careful, I kept using it. That small compromise became an expensive mistake.

I also was not patient enough when the spill happened. I reacted too quickly and did not handle the situation as calmly or carefully as I should have. If I had slowed down, protected the machine properly, and made better decisions in the first few minutes, the damage might not have been as bad.

## What this is really teaching me

This is not only about a laptop. It is about how small habits create bigger outcomes.

- A loose lid can become a $2,500 repair.
- A rushed reaction can make a bad situation worse.
- A repeated warning that I ignore will eventually become a consequence.

I do not want to keep paying for avoidable mistakes with money, time, or regret. I want to become more stable, more careful, and more deliberate in the way I do things.

## The decision

From today, I will treat small risks seriously. If something is unreliable, I will fix it, replace it, or remove it before it becomes a bigger problem.

I will also work on my mindset: slower reactions, cleaner routines, and more certainty in the way I manage my environment.

This matter stops here. Moving forward.
